The Problem

Every assessment tool built for teachers was designed to satisfy someone above the teacher. Grades go up to the gradebook. Scores go up to the district. Reports go up to the state. The teacher is the data entry point, not the beneficiary.

Meanwhile, the most important thing a teacher knows — how a student showed they understood something — lives nowhere. It doesn't survive the week, let alone a parent meeting, an IEP review, or a conversation with a principal who needs to explain a test score.

That knowledge is the evidence. And right now, it evaporates.

What the other tools do — and don't

Seesaw
Captures photos and videos of student work
Doesn't tag how understanding appeared or map it to standards
MagicSchool
Generates lesson plans and rubrics fast
AI before engagement — creates content, doesn't document learning
Formative
Captures real-time student responses mid-lesson
Snapshots only — no longitudinal thread, no expressive pathway
MasteryConnect
Tracks standards mastery by assessment score
District dashboard first — teachers get a read-only window
Khanmigo
Socratic AI tutoring for students
AI before sense-making — substitute, not scaffold
ClassDojo
Behavior tracking and parent communication
Not designed to document academic understanding at all
This tool
Documents how understanding appeared — verbal, embodied, narrative, reflective — and gives you the professional language to record it, report it, and defend it upward

What it does

01

Expressive pathway documentation

Tag how understanding appeared — verbal explanation, physical demonstration, narrative framing, peer teaching, reflective articulation. Not just that the student got it. How it came through.

02

Classroom Signals vocabulary — built in

Pre-loaded with standardized professional language drawn from the assessment visibility framework. You pick the phrase that fits; the documentation writes itself in language that survives a meeting.

03

Standards alignment

Tag observations to NM academic standards — CCSS ELA and Math, NGSS science, NM Social Studies, Native American Studies, Fine Arts, and IDEA/IEP frameworks. Built for New Mexico's actual requirements, including the equity obligations from Yazzie/Martinez.

04

Administrator language — on demand

When you need to explain to a principal, a district coordinator, or a parent why a student who failed the test actually understands the material — the tool generates the language. Not jargon. Defensible professional framing.

05

AI as secondary scaffold — after engagement

AI support is available for reflection and articulation after the student has engaged with the material. Not before. Never as the first voice in the room.

The framework behind it

"Recognizing multiple expressive pathways does not lower standards. It refines what counts as evidence."

This tool is the operational interface to Assessment Evidence and Expressive Pathways in AI-Present Schools (Campbell, 2026, CC BY 4.0 / Emerging Rule). The framework argues that contemporary assessment systems are not too lenient — they are insufficiently sensitive. Students who understand are being under-seen. The fix is not to lower the bar. It is to widen what counts as evidence that the bar was cleared.

Grounded in Montessori-informed pedagogy, aligned with ESSA's multiple-measures provisions and New Mexico's Yazzie/Martinez equity obligations. No product endorsements. No surveillance. Teacher judgment is central and irreplaceable.
