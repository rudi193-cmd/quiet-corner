# Appendix C — Ethics, Access, and Equity Reference Notes

Expanded notes supporting §5 of the [white paper](white-paper.md). These are **design constraints**, not compliance checklists.

---

## 1. Equity without lowering standards

**Principle:** Equity here means **accurate recognition** of understanding across diverse expressions—not reduced expectations or tracked remediation based on expressive preference alone.

**Practice:**

- Hold **one learning goal** across pathways ([Appendix B](appendix-b.md))
- Document **evidence**, not **traits**
- Avoid language that medicalizes, diagnoses, or labels learners in assessment records

---

## 2. Access and expressive pathways

Not every student has equal access to every medium (technology, instruments, home recording environments, materials for performance). Pathway expansion must include **equitable access to the means of demonstration**, not only permission to demonstrate.

**Questions for teams:**

- Can each pathway be completed **during school** if home resources are uneven?
- Are we adding pathways that **advantage** students with more external resources?
- Do we provide **scaffolds** (time, space, materials) without lowering the cognitive demand?

---

## 3. AI, transparency, and governance

**Transparency** in this framework means:

- Clear **purpose** for tool use (reflection, clarification, practice—not substitution)
- Clear **limits** (after engagement; teacher judgment final)
- Clear **accountability** (what gets documented)

**Transparency is not:**

- Constant monitoring of keystrokes
- Surveillance framed as "integrity"
- Automated scoring replacing professional judgment

---

## 4. Misinterpretation of learner capacity

False negatives harm learners when:

- Narrow evidence is treated as **complete** evidence
- Behavioral observations in one setting are conflated with **content knowledge**
- Polished output is treated as **deep understanding** without process evidence

Structural mitigation: **multiple pathways**, **calibration**, **shared language** ([Classroom Signals Guide](classroom-signals.md)).

---

## 5. What this framework explicitly rejects

| Rejected approach | Why |
|-------------------|-----|
| Deficit labeling in assessment records | Obscures evidence; harms equity |
| Diagnostic inference from performance | Outside teacher assessment role; ethical risk |
| Tool-first pedagogy | Cognition must precede scaffold |
| AI as grading authority | Removes professional judgment |
| Ban-only AI policy without assessment redesign | Treats symptom; leaves blind spot |

---

## 6. Institutional communication

When discussing equity with administrators, frame pathway expansion as:

- **Accuracy improvement** — fewer wrong negatives
- **Integrity preservation** — evidence tied to learning goals
- **Governance** — purpose and limits on AI, not detector theater
